Quantum Mechanics: Copenhagen Interpretation, Wave Function, and Heisenberg's Uncertainty Principle



Before Erwin Schödinger's thought experiment involving the famous "Schrödinger Cat", there was a physics theory which truly set the ground work for Quauntum Mechanics and the understanding of light as a particle or wave. This theory is known as the Copenhagen Interpretation. In this interpretation we are introduced to a mathematical function called the wave function. The Wave Function helps physicists to tackle experiments in an entirely different perspective!

How did the Wave Function come about? Initially Heisenberg's Uncertainty Principle brought about the idea that when a particle's place is known, the momentum is inconsequential. To better understand this we can imagine a basketball in mid air; the more we know of the basketball's exact position, the less we know about how the basketball's position is changing. This uncertainty principle is a key point in why the wave function determines light the way it does. The wave function determines that when light is observed by an experimental device the light will collapse from a wave into a particle, this is also known as the collapse of the wave function. Pretty neat right? It gets better!


With this conclusion made we can assume the existence of wave-particle duality. An experiment which displays this concept beautifully is known as the double slit experiment. On a singular scale, when a single electron is shot through two slits that have a width smaller than said electron, the electron will diffract. Diffraction describes how the electron ends up warping and behaves like a wave. However, we are discussing quantum objects which behave as a wave until observed. Upon observation which occurs near the slits, the wave function collapses and our quantum object behaves as a particle. We do not know which slit the quantum object will pass through because at this point our it is in superposition. Probability works wonders in making this determination for us! This in essence, is the Copenhagen Interpretation!
